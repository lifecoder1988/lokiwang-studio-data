I've built a lot of features — and later deleted them with my own hands. Hitting delete always stings a little. But live with a product long enough and you realize: users never remember how much you added. They remember whether they could get one thing done without friction.

## Less, But Better

When you maintain a product alone and every line of code is your own responsibility, you feel it in your bones — every extra feature is another debt you'll be tending for a long time.

> "Good design is as little design as possible."

So I gave myself a rule: if I can't say in one sentence who a feature is for and what problem it solves, it doesn't get built. Not yet.
