# I Had Claude Run a Car Launch Keynote. The Slides Nearly Broke Me.

People keep asking me lately whether Claude can actually do any "real work"…

As it happens, I had the perfect excuse. I'd been wanting to find a genuinely nasty test to put it through: **build the keynote deck for a car company's product launch.** What makes this job so devilish is that it grades three things at once — taste, information architecture, and that peculiar *emotional* charge every good launch event has. Miss by a hair and you get a village loudspeaker; nail it, and it's a keynote.

So I handed it a made-up brand: LUMEN — LUMEN Motors — and told it to launch a flagship electric sedan, the L7.

Then I flipped through what it handed back… and honestly, the moment that first slide's slogan lit up, I was already starting to lose it.

## 01 The Open: "Three Years. One Car."

The first slide of any launch is where skill shows most — and where things crash hardest.

How do most people (and most AI) write it? "LUMEN L7 Smart Electric Sedan — Class-Leading Specs Across the Board." Done. Press-release stench everywhere, and the audience is already reaching for their phones.

Here's what it gave me instead:

> **Three years. One car.**
> From the first sketch to the ten-thousandth unit off the line, LUMEN has been answering a single question: what should the next decade of electric cars look like?

…I have to admit, that's got the flavor. One short line lands to establish authority; one long line spells out the *obsession*. And the closer — "**Born for the light**" — casually pulls the double meaning of the name LUMEN back around and ties it off.

On the strength of that open alone, I'd say this deck is already halfway to winning.

## 02 The Hard Numbers: It Actually Knows *How* to Hype

The hardest part of a launch isn't listing specs — it's **knowing which number deserves to be blown up.**

In this deck, it didn't smear every spec across your face. One slide, one hero, each with a number that ticks up in animation:

- Drag coefficient **Cd 0.198**, immediately followed by "every 0.01 Cd cut ≈ 8 km more range" — see, it knows that just tossing out 0.198 does nothing for you; you only go "ohhh" once it's **translated into range**;
- Performance slide: combined **673 PS**, 0–100 in **2.9 seconds**, top speed **265 km/h** — three numbers, one slide, clean and sharp;
- Powertrain slide: full-domain **900V** silicon-carbide high-voltage platform, in-house motor control, integrated three-in-one electric drive — the jargon is stacked up like a real pro's.

The slide that really got me was charging. It didn't write "charges fast." It wrote:

> 10% → 80%, **the time it takes to drink a coffee**, about 500 km back in the tank.

Plus a line: "Beijing → Shanghai, one charge stop the whole way."

That's the essence of launch-speak: **don't report the number, report the feel.** An AI coming up with the idea to translate "charging C-rate" into "one coffee" all on its own — that genuinely surprised me.

## 03 Cabin and Autonomy: It Speaks the Industry Slang Too

Flip further and you hit the standard-issue segments of every launch these past two years — it didn't skip a single one, and it talks the talk fluently:

- Cabin: a **3.2K wraparound triple-screen** (45-inch integrated curved display), an onboard large-model assistant with "wake-word-free, millisecond continuous dialogue," and phone·home·car "one account, seamless handoff";
- Autonomy: **end-to-end single neural network**, "straight from perception to trajectory," drivable nationwide, a TOPS-class compute platform, LiDAR included, trained on massive real-world scenario data.

"End-to-end," "single neural network," "drivable nationwide"… the way it arranges these phrases is cut from almost exactly the same cloth as this year's real launch events. It's clearly digested the industry's corpus.

## 04 Safety and Price: The Final Tap-In on the Last Slide

The safety slide plays it steady: hot-formed steel cage body, dual-sided liquid-cooled battery, passing every test — nail penetration, crush, burn — far-side airbag plus full-cabin side curtains. Nothing daring, but the "sense of security" a launch is supposed to give? Delivered.

The real tap-in comes at the very end: **three colors, two trims, and the price.**

- L7 RWD Long Range: **from ¥239,800**
- L7 Max AWD Performance: **¥279,800**
- "Tonight at 21:00 · Pre-orders open across all channels"

It even lined up the "pre-orders open tonight at 21:00" urgency-manufacturing closer… it has genuinely learned the whole **playbook structure** of a launch: premise → design → performance → charging → cabin → autonomy → safety → pricing → the hard close. Not a single beat missing.

## 05 So — Can It Actually Do It?

Having flipped through the whole thing, here's my overall take: **the information architecture and the copywriting are close to usable; pure taste still depends on how much work you put into your prompt.**

Its strongest quality is precisely the least "AI" thing about it — it understands **restraint.** It knows to make each slide say one thing. It knows to translate specs into feel. It knows to open with a short line and close with a pun. You can't recite your way to that off a spec sheet — that's *understanding launches.*

That said, don't rush to crown it. This is, after all, a **fictional** car — the specs are ones it made up to hang together, with no engineering or supply chain backing them up. Actually putting it on stage to face media Q&A would be another matter entirely… (we'll leave that one aside for now).

But as a launch deck taken "from zero to ready-to-show," here's my verdict:

**When it comes to launch decks, the question with Claude is no longer whether it can make one — it's whether you dare to just use it as-is.**
