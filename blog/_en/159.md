## 03 One Sentence, New Scene: The Battlefield Moves to the Hall of Supreme Harmony

At 16:35 I sent sentence number three:

> Swap the scene for the Forbidden City.

Sounds like swapping a background image. It isn't.

In the Forbidden City asset pack, the Hall of Supreme Harmony comes disassembled into a whole suite of GLBs — exterior, collision, interior, roof, beams and columns, doors, terrace base. To make it actually fightable, three things had to be handled:

**First, exterior and collision have to line up.** The visible model handles looking good; the collision pack gets converted to `StaticBody3D` and handles keeping you from walking through walls.

**Second, the hall interior can't all be crammed into memory up front.** Only when the player approaches the Hall of Supreme Harmony does `ResourceLoader.load_threaded_request()` async-load the interior in the background; walk away for 8 seconds and it unloads.

**Third, the combat arena needed recomposing.** The boss guards the base of the white marble stairs, the Meridian Gate sits as a distant southern backdrop, two corner towers anchor east and west, and Cao Wei banners, barricades, and braziers carve the empty plaza into layers.

## 05 Three Real Pitfalls: Stairs, Sleep Mode, and a Vanishing Face

What actually ate the time were these three problems.

### 1. The stairs of the Hall of Supreme Harmony were unwalkable

I said "after the battle, walk into the hall".

It sent the bot toward the stairs.

The bot then got stuck, firmly, at the very first step…

Root cause: the terrace base in the asset pack is essentially one solid **82×6.2-meter collision box**, with no gap left for the central stairway; the original ramp collision's lower edge also floated 0.6 meters off the ground.

The fix: rebuild the left and right terrace bases as separate BoxShapes, carve the middle open, and slide in an invisible 33° ramp. The ramp lives on its own collision layer 2 — the character can step on it, the camera's spring arm doesn't collide with it.

![The bot climbing the central imperial stairway on the rebuilt invisible ramp](/api/media/uploads/2026/07/1784289184583-climb-stairs.jpg)

It looks like just "walking up the stairs".

Behind it was full-on collision-body surgery…

### 2. At 40 seconds in, every recording froze

The recording was even spookier.

Frame-by-frame screenshots, live screen capture, Movie Maker — didn't matter. The picture froze at roughly the 40-second mark; game logic and audio kept marching on.

It finally diffed viewport texture hashes to pin the root cause: **after the macOS display sleeps, Metal stops updating the render viewport of occluded windows.**

So the final command ended up looking like this:

```bash
WOLONG_MOVIE=1 caffeinate -d godot --path . \
  --write-movie out.avi --fixed-fps 30
```

`caffeinate -d` keeps the display awake, and the game window auto-pins itself on top during recording; Godot's Movie Maker renders frames offline, and the audio comes straight from the engine's actual mix.

Later I asked it: "Can you record in the background?"

It tried `--headless`. Turns out Godot 4.6 in macOS headless mode can't get the Movie Writer's render texture — it errors out with a null parameter.

So "background recording" on this machine actually means: the background task runs unattended, but there still has to be an always-on-top window on screen that must not be minimized.

How to put it… very background, yet not entirely background.

### 3. I wanted to see the face — the character vanished instead

At 18:28 I swapped in a human-proportioned Hyper3D character, then said:

> After the fight, do a 360 orbit, because I want to see the character's face clearly.

In the first two test recordings, whenever the camera got close, the character flickered in and out of existence.

The model wasn't broken — this Mixamo-retargeted skinned mesh reported an inaccurate **AABB bounding box**. Tilt the camera slightly and Godot decided the character had left the view, and frustum-culled it on the spot.

The fix was adding `extra_cull_margin = 8.0` to every `MeshInstance3D` — only then did the character deign to stay in frame.

![After victory the camera drops to face height, and the character finally stops being wrongly culled](/api/media/uploads/2026/07/1784289193847-orbit-face.jpg)

Also, the human-proportioned model has longer legs, so the original run animation looked like a moonwalk in space. It sped `Running_A` up to 1.35× and `Running_B` to 1.8× so the cadence could catch up with the actual movement speed.

**Swapping a model is never just swapping a file.**

What you're swapping is a whole chain of debt: bounding boxes, stride length, animation timing, and camera distance…

## 08 So

Looking back at those 6 sentences, I realized they weren't about "writing great prompts".

Each one was actually a trade-off:

- Don't chase realism yet — get the core loop running first;
- Don't just tell me it's playable — record it and show me;
- The wasteland is too generic — make it the Forbidden City;
- Don't cut to black after the win — walk into the hall;
- New model? Then bring the camera close, no hiding;
- And finally, make producing the video itself a repeatable process.

The AI expanded each sentence into hundreds of actions.

But deciding what the next sentence should be — that was still the human.

That might be the most honest division of labor in AI projects right now: **it writes the code, it steps in the pits, but the direction has to come from me, one sentence at a time.**

$54 didn't buy me Wo Long 2.

It bought 6 judgment calls, all finally turned into footage.
